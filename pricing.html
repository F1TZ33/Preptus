Pricing and multipliers

Pricing is based on a base package cost multiplied by factors such as revenue, complexity, implementation risk, and urgency. This allows pricing to scale with the real demands of each engagement while maintaining structured consistency.

Illustrative multiplier model

FactorIllustrative rangeWhat it captures
Revenue scale1.0x – 1.8xOrganisational size and commercial weight
Complexity1.0x – 1.6xOperational moving parts and structural difficulty
Implementation risk1.0x – 1.5xChange pressure, execution risk, and delivery burden
Urgency1.0x – 1.4xResponsiveness and timeline compression

Simple formula

Indicative fee = Base package × revenue factor × complexity factor × risk factor × urgency factor

This page is intentionally public-facing in tone. It communicates that pricing is structured and commercially disciplined without exposing every internal pricing decision.
